About

Hi, my name is Connor, and I built this website using HTML, CSS and a bit of JavaScript during my spring semester of college in 2025. On this web page, I'd like to tell you a bit about myself.

I grew up in the relatively small town of Traverse City, MI. I went to high school at Saint Francis High School, a small private catholic high school. While I was there, I discovered my love for music, I deepened my faith, and I was first exposed to coding in my AP Computer Science class junior year. Throughout high school, I was very involved, including 4 years of marching and concert band, 4 years of cross country, 4 years acting in the school musical, 3 years in track and field, and one year in the school choir. I also formed a cover band with some friends in my junior year, as the bassist. When applying for colleges, I applied to The Catholic University of America (CUA) as an Architecture major. I was accepted and began studying architecture as a freshman in 2023. However, I soon discovered that Architecture was not my passion, and in the spring of 2024 I switched to Computer Science. While I was attending CUA, I was a member of the Cornerstone Scholars program, made the dean's list every semester, and was the co-captain of the pep band '24-25.

Between Spring and Summer 2025, I transfered from CUA to the University of Dayton, still as a computer science major. I am now attending UDayton as a Junior, and have become involved in the University Jazz Band, and a card-game club. I also formed yet another cover band but with some of my new friends I met.

As for my interests and hobbies, I enjoy playing my bass, learning new instruments, going to concerts, playing video games, reading books, playing board/card games, and running.

During the summers, I work as a floor salesman at a shoe store, a local small business that is family-owned and operated.

I am yet to secure an internship, but I am in the market for one as we speak!
